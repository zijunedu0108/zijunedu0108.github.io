14.1 Infectious Diseases

STPM SEM 2 FULL MARKS GUIDE

📌 1. Essential Definitions

Infectious Disease: A disease caused by an invading pathogen transmitted from an infected organism through a medium (water, air, vector) leading to bodily reaction.

Contagious: Easily transmitted via direct or indirect contact.

Pathogen (病原体): A infectious agents that cause disease (include virus, bacteria, fungi, protozoans, helminths).
Carrier (带原者): A person who harbours the pathogen but shows no signs or symptoms.

🚀 3. Transmission Mechanisms

• Indirect: Via vectors (e.g., Aedes mosquito, Tsetse fly, Ticks).

• Direct: Bites/scratches (Rabies, CSD) or Body fluids (HIV, Syphilis).

• Vertical: Mother to fetus via placenta or breast milk (HIV, Zika, Herpes).

• Airborne: Droplet infection (SARS-CoV-2, TB).

📊 4. Spread Patterns

📍 Endemic: Consistently present, limited region (Leptospirosis).

📈 Epidemic: Unexpected increase in a specific area (SARS 2003).

🌍 Pandemic: Geographically widespread / Worldwide (COVID-19).

🛡️ 6. Vaccination Strategy

Type of Immunity: Artificial Active Immunity

Smallpox Eradication: Success due to Vaccinia virus vaccine (live-attenuated) + No animal reservoir + Visible symptoms.

Herd Immunity (群体免疫): Achieved at 95% vaccination rate. Protects the vulnerable (babies/elderly).

📝 Essay: How Vaccine Prevents Smallpox

  1. Vaccine contains weakened vaccinia virus.
  2. Recognised by lymphocytes as foreign antigen.
  3. Stimulates humoral immune response.
  4. B cells proliferate into antibodies and memory cells.
  5. Memory cells circulate for long-term protection.
