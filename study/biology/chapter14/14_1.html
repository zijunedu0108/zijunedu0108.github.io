14.1 Infectious Diseases

1. Fundamental Concepts (基本概念)

Infection (感染):

Process of invasion of pathogens, where they multiply and replicate in the body[cite: 7].

Pathogens (病原体):

Causative agents that cause disease, including viruses, bacteria, fungi, protozoans, and helminths[cite: 10, 11].

Vectors (媒介):

Organisms (usually arthropods) that carry pathogens from an infected organism to a person[cite: 12].

2. Transmission Modes (传播方式)

ModeDescription & Example
Direct
(直接)		 Contact with body fluids or animal scratches (e.g., HFMD, AIDS, CSD)[cite: 18, 23, 24].
Indirect
(间接)		 Via vectors like mosquitoes (e.g., Dengue, Malaria)[cite: 15, 16, 17].
Airborne
(空气)		 Inhaling contaminated air droplets from coughs/sneezes (e.g., SARS-CoV-2, TB)[cite: 25, 33].
Vertical
(垂直)		 From mother to foetus via placenta or breast milk (e.g., HIV, Zika)[cite: 34, 35, 39].
Waterborne
(水源)		 Consuming contaminated food or water (e.g., Cholera, Hepatitis A)[cite: 40, 42].

3. Vaccination (疫苗接种)

A biological preparation of weakened or inactive pathogens to stimulate the immune system[cite: 80].

Live Vaccine (减毒活疫苗): Weakened pathogens; long-term protection; not for weak immune systems (e.g., MMR, Smallpox)[cite: 82, 83].
Non-live Vaccine (灭活疫苗): Dead pathogens; safer for weak immune systems; requires boosters (e.g., Hepatitis A, Flu)[cite: 84, 85, 86].

4. Patterns of Spread (传播模式)

  • Endemic (地方性): Consistently present in a specific region[cite: 48].
  • Epidemic (流行性): Unexpected increase in a specific area[cite: 50].
  • Pandemic (大流行): Widespread across continents or worldwide[cite: 53, 54].

Self-Test (自我检测)

Q: Describe how vaccination helps to prevent smallpox.

1. Vaccine contains weakened vaccinia virus[cite: 129].
2. Lymphocytes recognize it as a foreign antigen[cite: 129].
3. Stimulates humoral immune response: B cells produce antibodies and memory cells[cite: 130].
4. Memory cells circulate to protect against future infection[cite: 131].
