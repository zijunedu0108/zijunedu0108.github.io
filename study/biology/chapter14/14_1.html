14.1 Infectious Diseases

1. Fundamental Concepts (基本概念)

Infection (感染) The process of invasion of pathogens, where they multiply and replicate in the body[cite: 7].
Pathogens (病原体) Also known as causative agents. Includes: viruses, bacteria, fungi, protozoans, and helminths (e.g., tapeworms)[cite: 10, 11].
Contagious Disease (接触传染病) A type of infectious disease easily transmitted through direct or indirect contact[cite: 9].
Notifiable Disease (法定报告传染病) Diseases that require mandatory reporting to the Ministry of Health (e.g., dengue, malaria, TB, HFMD).

2. Identification (体征与症状)

Signs (体征) Visible expressions detected by a doctor (e.g., rash, fever, blisters)[cite: 46].
Symptoms (症状) Only reported by the patient (e.g., pain, headache, nausea)[cite: 47].

Incubation Period: Time between infection and appearance of symptoms[cite: 43].

Infectious Period: Time when the person can spread the disease[cite: 45].

3. Modes of Transmission (传播途径)

ModeDescription & Examples
IndirectVia vectors (arthropods). Ex: Aedes (Dengue), Anopheles (Malaria), Tsetse fly (Sleeping sickness)[cite: 16, 17, 63].
DirectBites/scratches from animals (Rabies, CSD) or body fluids (AIDS, Syphilis, HFMD)[cite: 22, 24, 63].
AirborneInhaling droplets from sneezing/coughing. Ex: SARS-CoV-2, Tuberculosis (TB)[cite: 33, 63].
VerticalMother to child via placenta, parturition, or breast milk (AIDS, Zika, Herpes)[cite: 36, 37, 39, 63].
WaterborneConsuming contaminated food or water. Ex: Cholera, Hepatitis A, Salmonella[cite: 42, 70].

4. Patterns of Spread (传播模式对比)

  • Endemic: Consistently present but limited to a region (e.g., leptospirosis in N. Sembilan)[cite: 48, 49].
  • Epidemic: Unexpected increase in cases in a specific area (e.g., SARS 2003, JE Nipah)[cite: 50, 51, 52].
  • Pandemic: Geographically widespread (worldwide) and uncontrolled (e.g., COVID-19, Swine flu)[cite: 53, 54, 55].

5. Vaccination & Immunity (疫苗与免疫)

A vaccine stimulates the immune system to produce artificial active immunity[cite: 80].

Live Vaccine: Weakened pathogens. Long-term protection. Not for weak immune systems (e.g., Smallpox, MMR)[cite: 82, 83].
Non-live Vaccine: Dead pathogens. Safer for weak immunity. Needs boosters (e.g., Hepatitis A, Flu, JE)[cite: 84, 85, 86, 87].
Herd Immunity: Develops if 95% of the population is vaccinated. Protects babies, elderly, and the ill[cite: 115, 117].

6. Case Studies (重点疾病案例)

Smallpox (天花): Caused by Variola virus. Vaccine uses Vaccinia virus (live)[cite: 97, 100]. Eradicated in 1980[cite: 102].
MMR Vaccine: Protects against Measles (Paramyxovirus), Mumps, and Rubella[cite: 103, 104, 105].

Q3: Describe how vaccination helps to prevent smallpox.

Show Full Answer
1. Vaccine contains weakened vaccinia virus[cite: 129].
2. Recognized by lymphocytes as foreign antigen, stimulating a humoral immune response[cite: 129, 130].
3. B cells proliferate and divide to produce antibodies and memory cells[cite: 130].
4. Memory cells circulate for long-term protection against future infection[cite: 131].
