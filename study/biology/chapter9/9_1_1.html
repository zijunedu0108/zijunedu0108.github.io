9.1.1 Transport System & Heart

STPM Biology Full Guide

🫀 1. Structure of Mammalian Heart

  • Location: Cone-shaped, between lungs, beneath the sternum.
  • Pericardium: Double-walled sac. Protects, anchors, and prevents overfilling. Secretes pericardial fluid to reduce friction.
  • Myocardium: Muscular layer responsible for contractions. Thicker in ventricles (especially the Left Ventricle).
Important: The Septum separates the left and right sides to prevent the mixing of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.

🛡️ 2. Valves & Blood Flow

Valve TypeLocationFunction
Atrioventricular (AV)Between Atria & VentriclesPrevent backflow into atria.
SemilunarBase of Aorta & Pulmonary ArteryPrevent backflow into ventricles.

Tricuspid: Right side | Bicuspid (Mitral): Left side.

🔄 3. The Cardiac Cycle

A complete heartbeat lasts approx. 0.8 seconds.

1. Atrial Systole (0.1s): Atria contract, pushing the remaining 30% of blood into ventricles.
2. Ventricular Systole (0.3s):

• Isovolumetric Contraction: Ventricles contract, pressure rises, AV valves close (Lubb sound).

• Ventricular Ejection: Pressure exceeds arteries, Semilunar valves open, blood rushes out.

3. Relaxation / Diastole (0.4s):

• Isovolumetric Relaxation: Ventricles relax, pressure drops, Semilunar valves close (Dub sound).

• Ventricular Filling: Pressure falls below atria, AV valves reopen, blood flows passively (70%).

🔊 4. Heart Sounds (Lubb-Dub)

  • First Sound (S1 - "Lubb"): Caused by the closure of AV valves during ventricular systole. Lower pitch, longer duration.
  • Second Sound (S2 - "Dub"): Caused by the closure of semilunar valves during ventricular diastole. Higher pitch, shorter duration.
